About me

November 7, 1997, Oviedo, Asturias. It dawns under a cold, gray sky, as is customary. Hundreds of clouds embrace the city with the typical serenity of an Asturian autumn. It is not the ideal climate for a sunflower to grow, but it is the perfect setting for the arrival of a girl who, although born in silence, brings with her a promise: to turn the world upside down.

I have always been curious by nature. I have never been satisfied with knowing how things happen without looking for a reason why. Perhaps that's why the path of sciences seemed the most attractive to me.

With my insatiable desire to learn and my ambition to constantly improve, I decided to study industrial engineering despite the criticism about its difficulty (which even motivated me). During the 4 years of the Degree, I felt at home. I became one of the best students in my class, achieving several honors and some recognition for academic excellence. I couldn’t ask for more.

After that, I continued with my Master's, gaining independence for the first time. I lived and studied in Madrid for the first year and the second year I took it in Stockholm, through the Erasmus program. I don’t want to go into detail about my experience in Stockholm, as I could spend a lifetime talking about it, and that’s not the purpose of this website. Just to mention that it was the best time of my life so far and also the greatest turning point. I returned, supposedly having completed all my studies, but... what now?

We begin the long and controversial "work phase." It is that phase in which you may (I say “may” because it seems optimistic) find jobs for which it was unnecessary to study hard for 6 years, salaries that should be embarrassing to offer, poor management of new talents, temporary contracts with zero guarantees, imaginary development opportunities... But "that's the way it is" (I suppose).

I remember a conversation with my manager when I started my second job: "I feel like something is preventing me from shining", I told him. We concluded, without much conviction, that I simply needed more time. And after accumulating 3 years of professional experience, I decided to stop. I realized that this experience had made me grow a lot. And that’s precisely what helped me understand that I needed to stop. Because when the path was right, I had always shone.

Today, I am tremendously grateful for that risky decision that many did not understand. Many people see me as someone sociable, sweet, and cheerful from the outside. But my primary self is innovative, ambitious, rebellious, nonconformist, and brave. And I cannot let down my primary self.

So, after much contemplation, I knew I had found what was missing. A more disruptive, more technological, more powerful, more relevant approach. To delve into the heart of the business and immerse myself in the world of data.

And I knew it because as soon as I saw it, I began to shine again.
